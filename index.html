James Kole

It is difficult to maintain one's professionalism while discussing their biggest passion. So often I find myself lost down the rabbit hole of StackOverflow or freeCodeCamp. Even more often, I will come to my senses only to realize I've been rambling to a non-technologist for far too long about things they clearly have no interest in, though many still claim to appreciate my enthusiasm, if nothing else. All of this stems from one very apparent fact about me:
I love to code!
FizzBuzz, Fibonacci, infinite loops, interfaces, maps, variable names that are far, far too explicitly named, I love it all. It's confusing, and frustrating and fascinating and wonderful and I simply cannot get enough.

Currently, I am looking for a place to spark a career. I understand that many people just want a job, and there is nothing wrong with that, but for me it needs to be more than that. Years ago, my greatest mentor would go on and on about how shes never once had to work. By her own words, she has simply always done what she loves, and while I can't exactly claim the same, I'm hoping to at least come close by finding a team that mirrors my passion. I want to create. We all have our dream projects, of course, but I want to get out of my comfort zone. I am by no means an architect, yet I want to write programs that build cities. I'm not an artist, but I would love to write code that generates beautiful and unique works of art! I'm not a doctor, yet I want to write programs that identify diseases and allow for more effective treatment! I want to create, to discover, to innovate, and I want to learn. There is so much that we can do with coding, it truly is the future. I stubbornly want to be a part of that future.

At the moment, I am a full-stack software developer. I consider myself efficient in both front-end and back-end development, whether it be head down, fingers to the keyboard coding, or spending hours clicking around designing and redesigning a webapp. I am also a strong proponent of the idea that, especially when designing, it's important to have a little fun!

Should you happen to be in the market for a driven, enthusiastic, charismatic developer with a contagious "can do!" attitude, feel free to reah out via the email form below. You are also welcome to connect with me on LinkedIn.
